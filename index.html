What haven't we seen there?

According to polls, 95% of Russians dream of travelling somewhere, but only 36% plan to spend their holidays in their home country. They say, what haven't we seen here at home? In fact, Russia is a whole universe with the gentle seas of the south, the dense forests of the Sayan Mountains and the rugged ice of the Putorana plateau. And you can see all of these beauties without millions of dollars account, passports and hours-long flights. Like, for example, Vera Bashmakova is a brave young mum who took all her three children, put them in her Lada and drove 20,000 kilometres across her home country. There are some interesting places worthy of your holiday.

Altai Republic

url

YouTube

Altai is the one of the most beautiful places in Russia. First of all, because of the mountains: if you drive along the ridge, you will see slopes covered with pine trees, mountain rivers and lakes. And if you open the car window, you can get acquainted with the invisible miracle of the area - the mountain air.

Altai has a moderate climate, so it is best to come here in summer. This way you will see all the variety of local flora and fauna. In the forests of Altai roam elk, eagles fly over the ridges and roe deer graze on the plains. And the famous manulas are also inhabitants of the Altai region.

Winter Baikal

url

wiki/Lake_Baikal

Everyone knows Baikal as the largest lake in the world. Many people also know that it is the largest source of fresh water and one of the most beautiful places in Russia.

Of course, that's all good. But Baikal is also an ideal place for skijoring competitions. This is a sport where a skier ties himself to a motorbike, and the tandem tries to develop as much speed as possible on the ice. In March 2019, a world record of 197.011 kilometres per hour was set at the Baikal Mile Festival.

Karelia

url

http://vodlozero

Siberia ends not in the Urals, but in Karelia: the Siberian larch, which forms the taiga. Siberian larch does not grow west of Vodlozera. But here it grows the forests of the Karelian national parks because of the impenetrable bogs which have never known the axe. Some of the pine trees are more than half a millennium old. Touch a living thing that saw the sun before Ivan the Terrible saw it. In a virgin forest, you won't find a trail for a hundred kilometres. And on the the trees a few metres off the ground are marked with bear claws. So everyone knows who's boss.
